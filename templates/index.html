MedMalPrax: Understanding trends in medical malpractice in the US

The cost of medical malpractice cases affects everyone.

Over the last 3 decades, the number and cost of medical malpractice cases have been steadily increasing. The financial burden of handling this problem often falls on practitioners and medical institutions with occasional support from various state funds. Medical malpractice cases contribute to the increasing cost of healthcare in the US, and further necessitate the use of state and federal juridical resources. In order to alleviate this burden a range of policies have been implemented at the state level with varying degrees of success, which you can see in the map below. In this project, we attempt to provide an analysis of such policies and management approaches and make recommendations to states and institutions to aid them in lowering the risk and cost of malpractice cases. You will also find here an algorithm to help lawyers and patients estimate potential payouts based on the specifics of their case.
